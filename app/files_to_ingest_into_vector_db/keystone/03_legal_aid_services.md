## Protection From Abuse (PFA) Orders

### How to File for a PFA in Pennsylvania

A PFA (Protection From Abuse) order is a civil court order to help protect victims of domestic violence.

Who can get a PFA:
- Spouses or former spouses
- Current or former intimate partners
- Parents of a common child
- Family members by blood or marriage
- Current or former household members

What a PFA can do:
- Order the abuser to stop abusing you
- Order the abuser to stay away from you, your home, workplace, or school
- Grant you temporary custody of children
- Order the abuser to relinquish firearms
- Order the abuser to pay support

Duration:
- A final PFA order can remain in effect for up to 3 years (previously 18 months, extended by law)

How to file:
- Go to the Prothonotary's office in your county courthouse
- Fill out the petition forms (available online at PALawHelp.org)
- File the petition (no filing fee for PFA petitions)

Free legal help for PFA:
- MidPenn Legal Services: 1-800-932-0356

- DVS Legal Center (Lancaster): 717-299-1249
