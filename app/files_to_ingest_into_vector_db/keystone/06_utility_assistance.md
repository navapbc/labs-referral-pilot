## Pennsylvania LIHEAP (Low Income Home Energy Assistance Program)

Website: https://www.pa.gov/agencies/dhs/resources/liheap
Apply Online: https://www.compass.state.pa.us
Phone (LIHEAP Hotline): 1-866-857-7095 (Monday–Friday)
TTY: PA Relay at 711

What it is:
- State and federally funded program to help low-income households pay heating bills.
- Grants do not have to be repaid.

2025-2026 Application Period:
- December 3, 2025 through April 10, 2026
- Apply early – funds distributed first-come, first-served

Grant Amounts:
- Cash grants: $200 to $1,000 (based on household size, income, and fuel type)
- Crisis grants: $25 to $1,000 minimum/maximum

Income Eligibility (150% Federal Poverty Level):
- 1 person: $23,475/year
- 2 people: $31,725/year
- 3 people: $39,975/year
- 4 people: $48,225/year
- Add $8,250 for each additional person

Documents needed:
- Proof of household income (pay stubs, award letters, employer statements)
- Utility bills
- Proof of identity
- Additional documents if not a U.S. citizen or if heating is included in rent

How to apply:
1. Online at www.compass.state.pa.us
2. Paper application at your County Assistance Office
3. Call 1-866-857-7095

Processing time:
- Approximately 2 weeks for cash grants

Crisis Grants (for emergencies):
- Broken heating equipment or leaking lines
- Main heating source completely shut off
- Less than 15-day fuel supply
- Danger of utility termination

Source URLs (verified January 2026):
- https://www.pa.gov/agencies/dhs/resources/liheap
- https://www.pa.gov/services/dhs/apply-for-the-low-income-home-energy-assistance-program-liheap

## Electric Utility Assistance Programs

### PPL Electric Utilities – OnTrack Program

Phone: 1-800-342-5775
Website: https://www.pplelectric.com/Residential+Customers/

What it is:
- Income-based payment plan for PPL Electric customers.
- Offers reduced monthly payments, shutoff protection, and debt forgiveness.

Benefits:
- Reduced monthly electric payments based on income
- Protection from shutoffs while enrolled
- Debt forgiveness – PPL forgives portion of back debt with each on-time payment

Eligibility:
- PPL Electric residential customers
- Household income at or below 150% of Federal Poverty Level
- Must provide 3 months of income documentation

How to apply:
- Contact PPL at 1-800-342-5775
- Contact your local Community Action Agency

Related PPL Programs:
- **WRAP** (Weatherization): Energy education, efficient appliances, home weatherization (insulation, caulking)
- **Operation HELP**: Emergency assistance for customers up to 250% FPL experiencing severe hardship

Source URLs (verified January 2026):
- https://www.pplelectric.com/Residential+Customers/

### Met-Ed / Penelec / Penn Power / West Penn Power (FirstEnergy) – Customer Assistance Programs

Administered through: Dollar Energy Fund
Phone (Dollar Energy Fund): 1-888-282-6816
Website: https://www.dollarenergy.org/need-help/pennsylvania/

Programs:
- **Pennsylvania Customer Assistance Program (PCAP)**: Reduced payments for low-income customers
- **Hardship Program**: One-time assistance grants

Eligibility (Hardship Program):
- Total gross household income at or below 250% FPL
- Residential account (no commercial, industrial, or shared utility)
- Must apply for LIHEAP/CRISIS first if eligible
- Must have paid at least $150 on bill in past 3 months

Grant amounts:
- Up to $500 per utility per year
- Cannot cover security deposits
- Can cover reconnection fees

How to apply:
1. Contact your utility company
2. Complete referral to community agency or MyApp portal
3. Provide utility bill and income documentation

Source URLs (verified January 2026):
- https://www.dollarenergy.org/program/firstenergy-pennsylvania-hardship-program/

## Dollar Energy Fund (Statewide)

Phone: 1-888-282-6816
Website: https://www.dollarenergy.org/need-help/pennsylvania/

What it is:
- Nonprofit providing utility assistance grants for gas, electric, water, and wastewater.
- Over 40 years serving Pennsylvania low-income households.

Programs:
- Pennsylvania Hardship Program (one-time grants)
- Emergency assistance
- Coordination with utility company programs

Eligibility:
- Varies by utility company (typically 150-250% FPL)
- Must be residential customer
- Payment history requirements may apply

How to apply:
- Contact your utility company first
- They will refer you to appropriate program
- Provide utility bill and income documentation

Important warning:
- Dollar Energy Fund will NEVER ask for credit card, bank account, or routing numbers
- Will NEVER ask for payment over the phone

Source URLs (verified January 2026):
- https://www.dollarenergy.org/need-help/pennsylvania/

## Weatherization Assistance Program (WAP)

Website: https://www.pa.gov/agencies/dced/weatherization-assistance-program

What it is:
- Free home energy efficiency improvements for low-income households.
- Reduces energy costs and improves home comfort.

Services include:
- Insulation
- Air sealing
- Heating system repairs or replacement
- Energy education

Eligibility:
- Income at or below 200% FPL
- Priority given to elderly, disabled, and families with children

How to apply:
- Contact your local Community Action Agency
- Apply when you apply for LIHEAP

Source URLs (verified January 2026):
- https://www.pa.gov/agencies/dced/weatherization-assistance-program

## Emergency Utility Shut-Off Prevention

### Pennsylvania Utility Shutoff Moratorium

What it is:
- Protection from utility shutoffs during winter months (December 1 – March 31) for certain customers.

Protected groups:
- Customers at or below 250% FPL
- Households with children under 12 months
- Medical certificate customers

Important:
- You may still owe money after the moratorium
- Contact your utility company to set up payment arrangements

Source URLs (verified January 2026):
- https://www.pa211.org/get-help/employment-expenses/electric-service-payment-assistance/
