# SECTION 16: SUBSTANCE USE & RECOVERY SERVICES

> **Crisis Resources:** If you or someone you know is in immediate danger, call **911**. For 24/7 substance use help, call the PA Get Help Now line at **1-800-662-HELP (4357)** or the national SAMHSA Helpline at **1-800-662-4357**.

## Tips for Getting Help

1. Start with a phone call – Call PA Get Help Now (1-800-662-4357) or your county drug and alcohol office if you're unsure where to begin.

2. Know your insurance – Many programs accept Medicaid, Medicare, and private insurance. County offices can help with funding if uninsured.

3. Consider your level of need:
   - Detox (medical supervision for withdrawal)
   - Residential/inpatient (live at facility)
   - Intensive outpatient (IOP) – several hours/week
   - Standard outpatient – weekly sessions
   - Peer support – ongoing community connection

4. Peer support is free – AA, NA, SMART Recovery, and other meetings cost nothing.

5. Recovery housing helps – Sober living environments improve long-term outcomes.

6. Involve your support system – Many programs offer family education and counseling.

7. Relapse is common but not required – If you've tried before, try again. Different approaches work for different people.
