## Federal Lifeline Program (Pennsylvania)

Phone: 1-800-234-9473 (USAC)
Email: LifelineSupport@usac.org
Website: https://lifelinesupport.org
Provider Search Tool: https://www.lifelinesupport.org/companies-near-me/
PA Public Utility Commission: 1-800-692-7380
PA PUC Website: https://www.puc.pa.gov

What it is:
- Federal program providing monthly discount on phone or internet service.
- Helps nearly 500,000 Pennsylvanians stay connected.
- Administered by PA Public Utility Commission (state) and FCC (federal).
- Only ONE Lifeline benefit per household.

Discount Amount (Pennsylvania):
- Voice service only: $5.25 per month
- Internet service: $9.25 per month
- Bundled phone/internet: $9.25 per month

Eligibility:
You qualify if your household income is at or below 135% of Federal Poverty Level OR if someone in your household participates in:
- Medicaid
- SNAP (Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program)
- Supplemental Security Income (SSI)
- Federal Public Housing Assistance
- Veterans Pension and Survivors Benefit

2025 Income Limits (135% FPL - approximate):
- 1 person: ~$20,383/year (~$1,699/month)
- 2 people: ~$27,681/year (~$2,307/month)
- 3 people: ~$34,979/year (~$2,915/month)
- 4 people: ~$42,277/year (~$3,523/month)
- Add ~$7,298/year for each additional person

Important Notes:
- Can use discount on either phone OR internet, not both
- Only one Lifeline benefit per household
- Must recertify eligibility annually
- If receiving free service, must use it regularly to stay enrolled

How to Apply:
1. Check eligibility at lifelinesupport.org
2. Gather required documents:
   - Proof of income (last 3 months pay stubs) OR
   - Proof of program participation (benefit letter, Medicaid card, EBT card)
   - Government-issued photo ID
3. Apply online at lifelinesupport.org or through a participating provider
4. Choose a Lifeline provider using "Companies Near Me" tool
5. Processing typically takes 2-4 weeks

Source URLs (verified January 2026):
- https://www.puc.pa.gov/telecommunications/telephone-tips/
- https://lifelinesupport.org
- https://www.puc.pa.gov/media/3595/lifeline-booklet_9_4_25.pdf

## IMPORTANT: Affordable Connectivity Program (ACP) ENDED

CRITICAL UPDATE - June 2024:
The Affordable Connectivity Program (ACP), which provided up to $30/month for internet service, ENDED in June 2024 due to lack of Congressional funding. The last fully funded month was April 2024.

What This Means:
- NO new ACP applications are being accepted
- Former ACP participants should:
  1. Apply for Lifeline program (up to $9.25/month)
  2. Contact internet provider about low-cost plans
  3. Check if provider offers transition discounts

Lifeline is NOT the same as ACP:
- Lifeline: Still available, smaller discount ($9.25/month)
- ACP: Ended, no longer available ($30/month discount is gone)

Beware of Scams:
Some websites still claim to offer ACP enrollment and collect personal information. Do NOT provide personal information to sites claiming ACP enrollment. The program ended June 1, 2024.

Source URLs (verified January 2026):
- https://www.puc.pa.gov/press-release/2024/puc-encourages-former-affordable-connectivity-program-customers-to-seek-other-assistance-options-to-access-the-internet

## Tips for Choosing a Lifeline Provider

Consider These Factors:
1. Coverage: Which provider has best coverage in your area? (Check coverage maps)
2. Network: T-Mobile, Verizon? Which works best where you live/work?
3. Data allowance: How much data is included in free plan?
4. Phone options: Does provider offer decent free phone? Can you bring your own?
5. Customer service: Read reviews about customer service quality
6. Additional costs: Can you add more data/minutes if needed? How much?

Popular Choices in Pennsylvania:
- SafeLink: Good for Verizon coverage areas
- Assurance: Good for T-Mobile coverage, generous data (25GB)
- Q Link: Good data allowances, easy SIM swapping

You Can Switch Providers:
- Not happy with your Lifeline provider? You can switch!
- Only one Lifeline service at a time, but you can change providers
- Contact new provider to transfer your Lifeline benefit
- Must de-enroll from old provider when switching

## Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can I have both Lifeline phone AND Lifeline internet?
A: No, only one Lifeline discount per household, applied to either phone OR internet service.

Q: What if multiple people in my household need phones?
A: Only one person per household can receive Lifeline. Others would need to pay regular rates or seek family plans.

Q: Do I have to accept the free phone?
A: No, most providers allow "Bring Your Own Phone" (BYOP) if your phone is compatible.

Q: Will my Lifeline phone work for 911?
A: Yes, all phones (including Lifeline phones) can call 911 even without minutes.

Q: How often do I need to recertify?
A: Annually. Your provider will contact you when it's time to recertify.

Q: What happens if I don't recertify?
A: You will be de-enrolled from Lifeline and lose the discount.

Q: Do I need to use my free phone regularly?
A: Yes, if you receive completely free service, you must use it regularly to stay enrolled.

Q: Can I keep my current phone number?
A: Usually yes - ask your chosen provider about porting your number.

Q: Is ACP still available?
A: No. The Affordable Connectivity Program ended June 1, 2024. Only Lifeline is currently available.
