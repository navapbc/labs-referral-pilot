## Medical Assistance Transportation Program (MATP)

Website: https://matp.pa.gov
State Info: https://www.pa.gov/services/dhs/medical-assistance-transportation-program

What it is:
- Non-emergency medical transportation at NO COST for Pennsylvania Medical Assistance (Medicaid) recipients.
- County-administered program.

Eligibility:
- Must have valid Medical Assistance card or be verified eligible
- Must be a resident of the county where service is requested
- Transportation must be for non-emergency medical treatment

Covered trips:
- Doctor appointments
- Dental appointments
- Mental health or drug & alcohol treatment
- Pharmacy visits for prescriptions
- Hospital or facility for tests or treatments
- Durable medical equipment suppliers

Transportation options:
- Shared ride vans
- Taxis
- Accessible vehicles
- Bus passes/reimbursement
- Mileage reimbursement (if using personal vehicle)

Special accommodations:
- Door-to-door service when medically verified
- Escorts allowed when medically necessary
- Children under 18 can have parent/guardian accompany at no cost

NOT covered:
- Emergency/ambulance transportation
- Non-medical trips (shopping, social activities)

How to apply:
- Contact your county MATP provider
- Complete application within 30 days of eligibility verification

County MATP Contacts:
- Dauphin County: rabbittransit, 717-238-8304
- Cumberland County: rabbittransit, 717-238-8304
- York County: rabbittransit, 717-846-7433
- Lancaster County: RRTA, 717-397-4246
- Lebanon County: Lebanon Transit, 717-274-3514
- Schuylkill County: STS, 570-429-2701 or 1-800-832-3322
- Northumberland County: rabbittransit, 717-238-8304
- Perry County: rabbittransit, 717-238-8304
- Juniata County: Contact County Assistance Office, 717-436-2158

Source URLs (verified January 2026):
- https://matp.pa.gov
- https://www.pa.gov/services/dhs/medical-assistance-transportation-program
