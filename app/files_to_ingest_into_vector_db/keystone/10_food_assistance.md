## Pennsylvania SNAP (Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program)

Apply Online: https://www.compass.state.pa.us
Phone: 1-866-550-4355
EBT Card Help: 1-888-328-7366
Website: https://www.pa.gov/agencies/dhs/resources/snap

What it is:
- Monthly food benefits on an EBT card.
- Can be used at authorized grocery stores and some farmers' markets.
- Formerly known as "food stamps."

Eligibility:
- Pennsylvania residents who meet income and household rules
- US citizens or eligible non-citizens
- Some adults may have work requirements
- People experiencing homelessness can apply (no fixed address required)

How to apply:
1. Apply online at www.compass.state.pa.us
2. Complete interview if scheduled (often by phone)
3. Submit requested documents
4. Watch for approval notice in mail or COMPASS portal

Documents needed:
- Photo ID or proof of identity
- Social Security numbers for household members
- Proof of income (pay stubs, benefits letters)
- Proof of address and household costs
- Immigration/citizenship documents (if applicable)

Processing time:
- Standard applications: Within 30 days
- Emergency situations: Within 7 days

Free SNAP Application Help:
- Central PA Food Bank SNAP Helpline: 1-877-999-5964
- Your local County Assistance Office
- Community Action Agencies

Source URLs (verified January 2026):
- https://www.pa.gov/agencies/dhs/resources/snap
- https://www.compass.state.pa.us

## Pennsylvania WIC (Women, Infants & Children)

Apply Online: https://www.pawic.com/OnlineApplication.aspx
WIC Hotline: 1-800-WIC-WINS (1-800-942-9467)
Website: https://www.pawic.com

What it is:
- Nutrition program for pregnant women, new mothers, infants, and children under 5.
- Provides healthy food benefits on a WIC card.
- Includes nutrition education and breastfeeding support.

Who can apply:
- Pregnant women
- Breastfeeding parents (up to 12 months after birth)
- Postpartum parents (up to 6 months after birth if not breastfeeding)
- Infants and children up to age 5
- Fathers, grandparents, and foster parents can apply on behalf of children

Eligibility:
- Pennsylvania resident
- Meet income guidelines (185% of Federal Poverty Level)
- Have a medical or nutritional risk
- If you receive Medicaid, SNAP, or TANF, you typically meet income guidelines

Income guidelines (effective July 2025):
- 1 person: $26,973/year
- 2 people: $36,482/year
- 3 people: $45,991/year
- 4 people: $55,500/year
- Add $9,509 for each additional person

How to apply:
1. Complete online pre-application at www.pawic.com
2. OR call 1-800-WIC-WINS
3. Schedule and attend a WIC appointment
4. Bring required documents

What to bring:
- Proof of identity (for you and child)
- Proof of address
- Proof of income (or proof of Medicaid/SNAP/TANF)
- Your child (for many appointments)

Local WIC Offices (contact for appointments):
- Dauphin County: Contact through PA WIC Hotline
- Lancaster County: Contact through PA WIC Hotline
- York County: Contact through PA WIC Hotline
- Lebanon County: Contact through PA WIC Hotline

Source URLs (verified January 2026):
- https://www.pawic.com
- https://www.pa.gov/agencies/health/programs/maternal-health-and-infant-care/wic

## Client Rights at Food Pantries

When receiving food assistance, you have the right to:
- Be treated with dignity and respect
- Receive food without discrimination
- Know what foods are available
- Have your privacy protected

---

## Tips for Visiting Food Pantries

1. Call ahead to confirm hours (schedules can change)
2. Bring reusable bags or a cart to carry food
3. Bring ID if required (many pantries do not require ID)
4. Ask about frequency limits (some pantries limit visits to once per month)
5. Ask about other resources available (SNAP application help, utility assistance)
