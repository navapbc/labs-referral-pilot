# SECTION 1: HOUSING & EMERGENCY SHELTERS

## Emergency Shelter System Overview

**How to access homeless services in South Central Pennsylvania:**
- For any county in the Goodwill Keystone service area, call **2-1-1** (PA 211) to connect with Coordinated Entry services.
- PA 211 navigators use standardized assessment tools to determine the level of housing need and provide referrals to shelters, rental assistance programs, and other community resources based on your location.
- You can call or text **211** toll-free from anywhere in the region, 24 hours a day, 7 days a week.

**If someone needs immediate help right now:**
- If there is an emergency (medical/safety), call **911**.
- For non-emergency housing assistance, call **2-1-1**.
- For domestic violence situations, call the National Domestic Violence Hotline at **1-800-799-7233** or contact a local YWCA hotline (see Domestic Violence section below).

**Source URLs (verified January 2026):**
- https://www.pa211.org/get-help/housing-shelter/housing-related-coordinated-entry/
- https://www.contacthelpline.org/
- https://pa211east.org/

### PA 211 – Housing-Related Coordinated Entry

Website: https://www.pa211.org/get-help/housing-shelter/housing-related-coordinated-entry/
Phone: 211 (call or text)

What it is:
- Coordinated Entry is the process by which anyone experiencing literal homelessness (or at imminent risk) can access all services and housing offered by the Continuum of Care network.

Who can access:
- People currently experiencing homelessness (in emergency shelter, in a place not meant for habitation, or fleeing domestic violence)
- Those at imminent risk of homelessness

How to access:
- Call or text 211 toll-free from anywhere in the region
- PA 211 navigators use standardized assessment tools to determine level of need
- Referrals provided to CoC agencies, shelters, rental assistance programs, and other community resources based on location and needs

Important notes:
- Coordinated Entry is available 24/7 through PA 211
- In-person assessments available at walk-in sites in each county (Community CARES in Cumberland County, HELP Ministries in Dauphin County, etc.)
