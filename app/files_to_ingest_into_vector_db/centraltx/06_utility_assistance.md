## City of Austin Customer Assistance Program (CAP) (Discounts, Plus 1, Medically Vulnerable Registry)

**Services / What they help with:**
- **Utility Bill Discounts** for eligible **City of Austin Utilities** residential customers (electric, water, wastewater, drainage, and more).  
- **Financial Support Plus 1** emergency utility help (funds are distributed through partner social service agencies).  
- **Services for the Medically Vulnerable** (registry + added protections/case management for customers with critical care needs).

**Who it’s for:**
- Utility Bill Discounts: customers who participate in certain assistance programs **or** meet income eligibility (the City offers a self-enrollment option for customers with household income at or below **200% of the Federal Poverty Guidelines**).
- Plus 1: **City of Austin utility customers** experiencing a temporary financial emergency (screened by partner agencies).
- Medically Vulnerable Registry: customers with critical care needs / life-support equipment and related eligibility requirements.

## Travis County Utility Assistance (includes CEAP)

**Services / What they help with:**
- Financial assistance for: **electricity, natural gas/propane, firewood (primary heat), water, and trash**.

**How it works (important notes):**
- Applications are served in the **order received** (waitlist).
- **Policy change (January 2025):** Households whose utilities have been **DISCONNECTED are now prioritized** for assistance. Previously submitted applications for households with disconnect notices are addressed in order received.
- If you're **currently disconnected**, Travis County asks you to **submit an application first**, then contact a community center for quicker help.

**Eligibility (documentation):**
- Valid photo ID, proof of Travis County residence, current utility bill(s), and proof of all household income (last 30 days for everyone 18+).  
- For federally funded CEAP assistance, additional documents may be required (proof of U.S. citizenship or eligible immigration status).

**How to apply:**
- **Online application portal (pre-screening + application):** https://tchhs.my.site.com  
- In person or by mail (paper application): contact/visit one of Travis County’s community centers (see Travis County Utility Assistance page).

**Income eligibility (CEAP):**
- Texas CEAP rules set income eligibility at or below **150% of the Federal Poverty Guidelines**.
- **Program Year 2025 (150% FPL) annual income limits (48 states & DC):**
  - 1 person: **$23,475**
  - 2 people: **$31,725**
  - 3 people: **$39,975**
  - 4 people: **$48,225**
  - For larger households: add **$8,250** per additional person (150% FPL increment).

**Benefit caps (state CEAP rules):**
- CEAP benefit determinations for Utility Payment Assistance / Crisis Assistance are capped on a sliding scale (per “component”), with maximums up to **$2,400 per component** depending on income band.
