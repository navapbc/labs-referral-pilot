## Central Texas Food Bank

**Address:** 6500 Metropolis Dr, Austin, TX 78744
**Phone:** (512) 282-2111
**Weather/Holiday Closures:** (512) 684-2559
**SNAP Application Help:** (855) 366-3401
**Website:** https://www.centraltexasfoodbank.org/
**Find Food Now Tool:** https://www.centraltexasfoodbank.org/find-food-now

**Organization Overview:**
- Largest hunger-relief charity in Central Texas
- ~250 partner organizations (food pantries, soup kitchens, shelters, senior programs)
- Provides immediate access to nutritious food
- Offers innovative programming and education
- Building new facility in Waco (announced March 2025) to better serve I-35 corridor

**Mission:** Nourish hungry people and lead the community in the fight against hunger

**Service Area (21 Counties):**
Travis, Williamson, Hays, Bastrop, Caldwell, Burnet, Blanco, Fayette, Lee, Llano, Coryell, Milam, and surrounding Central Texas counties

**Eligibility:**
- **No income verification required at most partner pantries**
- Simply show up during distribution hours
- May be asked to fill out short form confirming you live in area and need assistance
- Some specialized programs have additional requirements

**Services:**

**Partner Pantry Network:**
- ~250 partner organizations throughout 21 counties
- 95% of partner pantry food comes from CTFB
- Locations include food pantries, soup kitchens, homeless shelters, senior centers
- Use Find Food Now tool to locate nearest pantry

**Mobile Food Pantry:**
- Travels to different communities throughout service area
- Reaches people without reliable transportation
- Serves areas without grocery stores (food deserts)
- Multiple 18-wheeler trucks serving routes in Coryell, Milam, Travis, Hays, Williamson counties
- Fresh Food for Families distributions (fresh produce only at some sites)
- Monthly distribution calendars available on website

**SNAP Application Assistance:**
- Free phone and in-person consultations in English and Spanish
- Pre-screening and application submission help
- Call (855) 366-3401 or request appointment online
- Also helps with Medicaid, CHIP, TANF, Healthy Texas Women applications
- Partner agencies can also complete SNAP applications on-site

**Programs for Children:**
- Summer feeding programs
- Backpack programs during school year
- Child nutrition education

**Programs for Older Adults (Seniors):**
- Senior box programs
- Specialized nutrition for elderly
- Home delivery options through some partners

**Nutrition Education:**
- Cooking classes
- Healthy eating workshops
- Diabetes-friendly food options

**Workforce Training Programs:**
- Job training through Community Kitchen
- Employment readiness programs

**Client Rights:**
When receiving food from a Food Bank partner, you have the right to:
- Be treated with dignity and respect
- Receive food without discrimination
- Know what foods are available
- Have your privacy protected

**How to Access Food:**
1. Visit https://www.centraltexasfoodbank.org/find-food-now
2. Enter your ZIP code
3. View nearby pantries with days, times, and program details
4. **Call the site before visiting** to confirm hours (schedules can change)
5. Check for weather or holiday closures: (512) 684-2559
6. Bring reusable bags or cart to carry food home

**Important Notes:**
- Food pantry hours and schedules can change - always verify before going
- Assistance may be limited - bring your own bags/cart
- For help anytime: Dial 2-1-1 or call (877) 541-7905 for free, confidential, multilingual assistance

**Ways to Help:**
- Volunteer at warehouse, Community Kitchen, or Mobile Food Pantry
- Host virtual food drive (CTFB's purchasing power makes donations go further)
- Donate food
- Advocate for hunger relief policies

## SNAP (Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program)

**Apply Online:** https://www.yourtexasbenefits.com/  
**Apply by Phone:** Dial **2-1-1** (after selecting language, press **Option 2**) or call **(877) 541-7905**  
**Find Local Help / Offices:** https://www.211texas.org/  
**Lone Star Card (EBT) Help Line:** (800) 777-7328  

**What SNAP Provides:**
- Monthly money for groceries on the **Lone Star Card (EBT)** to buy eligible food items at approved stores.

> **IMPORTANT: SNAP Changes Effective April 1, 2026**
> Starting April 1, 2026, SNAP benefits will **NO LONGER cover**:
> - Candy (candy bars, gum, drops, taffy, chocolate-coated nuts/fruits)
> - Drinks with 5+ grams of added sugar per serving
> - Drinks with artificial sweeteners
>
> Additionally, expanded work requirements apply to adults ages 18-64 without dependents (must work or train 80 hours/month).
>
> **Source:** https://texasfosteryouthconnections.org/snap-benefit-restrictions/

**Eligibility (General):**
- Texas residents who meet **income and household rules** for SNAP.
- Must be a U.S. citizen or an eligible non-citizen (rules vary by immigration status).
- Some adults may have **work requirements** depending on household situation.
- If you're **experiencing homelessness**, you can still apply (no fixed address required).

**How to Apply / Access:**
1. Apply online through **YourTexasBenefits.com** (or use the Your Texas Benefits mobile app).
2. Complete the interview if HHSC schedules one (often by phone).
3. Submit requested documents (upload online/app, fax, or mail).
4. Watch for approval/denial notice and benefit start date in your portal/mail.

**Common Documents to Gather:**
- Photo ID (or other proof of identity)
- Social Security numbers for household members (if available)
- Proof of income (pay stubs, benefits letters, self-employment records)
- Proof of address and household costs (rent/lease, utilities)
- Immigration/citizenship documents (if applicable)

**Source URLs:**
- https://www.fns.usda.gov/state/texas  
- https://www.hhs.texas.gov/sites/default/files/documents/sample-form-h1020.pdf  
- https://www.211texas.org/

## WIC (Women, Infants & Children)

**Apply Online:** https://texaswic.org/apply  
**Statewide Phone:** (800) 942-3678  
**Austin Scheduling Center Phone:** (512) 972-4942  
**Austin Scheduling Center Hours:** Mon–Thu 7:30am–4:30pm; Fri 7:30am–1:30pm  
**Austin WIC Locations (current list):** https://www.austintexas.gov/page/wic-locations  

**What WIC Provides:**
- Monthly healthy food benefits on a **WIC card** (used at WIC-authorized stores)
- Nutrition education and support
- Breastfeeding support
- Referrals to healthcare and community services

> **WIC Card Update (2026):** New Texas WIC cards are being distributed **April–June 2026**. You must swipe your new card at stores starting **July 1, 2026**. Current chip cards remain valid through **June 30, 2026**.

**Who Can Get WIC:**
- Pregnant people
- Breastfeeding parents (up to 1 year after birth)
- Postpartum parents (up to 6 months after birth if not breastfeeding)
- Infants and children up to age 5
- Must live in Texas and meet income/nutrition criteria
- If you receive **Medicaid, SNAP, or TANF**, you typically meet WIC’s income guidelines automatically (still need an appointment)

**How to Apply / Access:**
1. Start your application online at **TexasWIC.org** or call (800) 942-3678.
2. In Austin/Travis County, you can also call the **Austin Scheduling Center** at (512) 972-4942 to set up an appointment.
3. Attend your WIC appointment (you’ll meet with a nutrition expert and complete required checks).

**What to Bring (Common Examples):**
- Proof of identity (for you and/or your child)
- Proof of address
- Proof of income (or proof of Medicaid/SNAP/TANF participation)
- Your child (for many appointments)

**Source URLs:**
- https://texaswic.org/apply  
- https://www.austintexas.gov/page/wic-locations  
- https://www.fns.usda.gov/contact/wic/texas

## Commodity Supplemental Food Program (CSFP)

**Administered locally by:** Central Texas Food Bank
**Phone:** (512) 282-2111 or toll-free (855) 366-3401
**Website:** https://www.centraltexasfoodbank.org/get-help/help-seniors

> **IMPORTANT: CSFP is for adults aged 60 and older ONLY.** This is not a general food assistance program.

**What It Is:**
- A USDA program providing one free monthly box of shelf-stable groceries (~25–30 lbs) to low-income seniors
- Box typically includes: cereal, shelf-stable milk, juice, peanut butter, dry beans/lentils, pasta/rice, canned meat/fish, canned fruits and vegetables, cheese, and grains

**Eligibility:**
- Must be **age 60 or older**
- Income at or below **130% of Federal Poverty Guidelines**:
  - 1 person: $20,345/year ($1,696/month)
  - 2 people: $27,495/year ($2,292/month)
  - 3 people: $34,645/year ($2,888/month)
  - 4 people: $41,795/year ($3,483/month)
- **Automatic income eligibility** if enrolled in SNAP, SSI, FDPIR, or Medicare LIS/MSP
- SNAP benefits do NOT count as income for CSFP

**Service Area:** 21 counties including Travis, Williamson, Hays, Bastrop, Caldwell, Burnet, Blanco, Fayette, Lee, and Llano

**How to Access:**
1. Use the **Find Food Now** tool at centraltexasfoodbank.org and filter for "Older Adult Programs"
2. Attend a distribution in person with **photo ID** (showing proof of age 60+) and income information
3. Complete a short application at the distribution site (valid for 3 years)
4. Pick up one box per month once approved

> **Note:** CSFP is first-come, first-served with limited boxes per site. Sites may maintain waiting lists.

**Related Program — HOPE (Healthy Options Program for the Elderly):**
- Also for ages 60+, but **no income proof required** (lower barrier than CSFP)
- Provides 15–20 lbs of shelf-stable groceries monthly
- Access through the same Find Food Now tool

**Source URLs (verified February 5, 2026):**
- https://www.centraltexasfoodbank.org/get-help/help-seniors
- https://www.fns.usda.gov/csfp/commodity-supplemental-food-program
- https://www.fns.usda.gov/csfp/eligibility-how-apply
