## Government Programs

### Travis County Reentry Resources

**Phone:** (512) 854-6497  
**Address:** 700 Lavaca Street, Suite 1530, Austin, TX 78701  
**Website:** https://www.traviscountytx.gov/reentry-resources  
**Reentry Resource Guide (PDF):** https://www.traviscountytx.gov/images/Reentry%20Guide%209.5.25.pdf

**Services offered:**
- **Job search assistance** — Help finding second-chance employers offering livable wage careers
- **Mock interviews and tips** — Individual or group sessions to practice interviewing skills
- **Resume building** — Assistance creating and refining resumes
- **Case management** — Minimum 6-month case management to navigate barriers
- **Emergency transitional housing** — Limited funds for temporary housing while seeking employment
- **Vital document recovery** — Help obtaining birth certificates, driver's licenses, Social Security cards, IDs
- **Educational tools** — Financial literacy, emotional intelligence, and soft skills training
- **Peer support** — Access to certified peer specialists (see Section 16 for peer support specialist training programs)
- **Vocational opportunities** — Information on free vocational training programs

**How to enroll:**
- Response time is typically 24–48 hours
- Services are voluntary (not mandatory)
- You do not have to live in Austin, but resources are primarily Travis County-based
- Can use services more than once

**Source URLs (verified January 15, 2026):**
- https://www.traviscountytx.gov/reentry-resources

### City of Austin – Fair Chance Hiring

**Website:** https://www.austintexas.gov/department/fair-chance-hiring

**What it is:**
The City of Austin has adopted fair chance hiring practices that limit when employers can ask about criminal history during the hiring process. This helps ensure people with criminal records have a fair opportunity to be considered for employment based on their qualifications.

## Replacing a Social Security Card

**Website:** https://www.ssa.gov/number-card/replace-card
**National Phone:** 1-800-772-1213 (Mon–Fri 8am–7pm) | TTY: 1-800-325-0778
**Cost:** Always free — there is no fee to replace a Social Security card.

**Online Replacement (adults only):**
- Available to U.S. citizens age **18 or older** with a state-issued ID from a participating state (Texas qualifies)
- Must have a "my Social Security" account at ssa.gov
- Must NOT be requesting any name or record changes
- **Children's cards CANNOT be fully replaced online.** You can start the application online, but you must finish in person at an SSA office.

**In-Person Replacement:**
- **Appointment required** since January 6, 2025. Call the local office or 1-800-772-1213 to schedule. Walk-ins accepted only for urgent/vulnerable cases.
- Bring **original documents** (photocopies and notarized copies not accepted):
  - **Adults:** Unexpired driver's license, state ID, or U.S. passport
  - **Children:** A birth certificate proves age/citizenship but is **NOT accepted as proof of identity**. Bring a school record, medical record, state ID, or U.S. passport for the child.
- Processing time: ~10 business days by mail
- Limits: 3 replacement cards per year, 10 per lifetime

## Tips for Successful Reentry

1. **Start planning before release** — Contact Travis County Reentry Resources or the TDCJ Reentry Hotline as early as possible
2. **Get your vital documents** — Birth certificate, Social Security card, and ID are essential for employment
3. **Be honest but strategic** — Many employers are willing to hire people with records; focus on your skills and what you've learned
4. **Use fair chance hiring resources** — Look for employers who have committed to fair chance hiring practices
5. **Build a support network** — Connect with peer support, faith communities, or mentors
6. **Address underlying issues** — Substance use treatment, mental health support, and stable housing improve outcomes
7. **Know your rights** — Some records can be expunged or sealed; consult legal aid for options
