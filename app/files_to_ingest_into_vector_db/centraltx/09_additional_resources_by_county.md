### Williamson County & Cities Health District (WCCHD) — Healthcare Helpline (WilCo Care)

**What they do:**
- Healthcare Helpline for Williamson County residents who are uninsured and need help figuring out potential healthcare resources (including the county’s indigent healthcare program, “WilCo Care”).

**How to access:**
- Call the Healthcare Helpline to discuss your situation and get screened / referred.

**Healthcare Helpline:** (512) 248-3252  
**Website:** https://www.wcchd.org/

**Eligibility notes (WilCo Care):**
- Must live in Williamson County, be 18+, not eligible for Medicaid, and meet strict income/resource limits
- Income must be at or below **21% of the Federal Poverty Level**
- Must be uninsured (no health insurance)
- Must have valid Social Security card, proof of income, and proof of residency

**What WilCo Care covers:**
- Office visits and annual physicals
- Diagnostic testing (lab and x-ray)
- Specialist care (with referral)
- Hospitalization (up to 30 days/year)
- Emergency room care
- Vision (eye exams and prescription eyewear, every 2 years)
- Prescriptions (limited to 3 medications/month, 30-day supply)
- Mental health services
- **Annual cap: $30,000** in eligible expenses

**What WilCo Care does NOT cover:**
- Dental services
- Hearing aids and durable medical equipment
- Chiropractic services
- Family planning
- Non-medically necessary procedures

**Source URLs (verified February 5, 2026):**
- https://www.wcchd.org/healthy_people/health_resources.php
- https://www.wcchd.org/services/program_eligibility_and_social_services/index.php
- https://www.wcchd.org/

## Hays County

### Hays-Caldwell Women’s Center (HCWC)

**Mailing Address:** P.O. Box 234, San Marcos, TX 78667-0234, Hays County  
**24/7 HELPline:** (512) 396-4357 or (800) 700-4292  
**Office Phone (business hours):** (512) 396-3404  
**Website:** hcwc.org  

**What They Offer:**
- 24/7 confidential crisis line for survivors of **family violence, dating violence, sexual assault, and child abuse**
- **Emergency shelter** (location is confidential for safety)
- **Counseling** (individual and group; services offered in English and Spanish)
- **Legal advocacy** and support navigating systems (courts, law enforcement, referrals)
- **Hospital accompaniment** through their emergency advocate response program  

**Eligibility / Who They Serve:**
- Survivors of abuse who **live, work, or go to school** in **Hays or Caldwell County**  
- Services are for **women, men, and children** and are free and confidential  

**How to Access:**
1. If you’re in danger, call **911**
2. Call the **24/7 HELPline** to talk with an advocate and ask about safety planning, shelter, counseling, or legal advocacy
3. For counseling intake/questions during business hours, call the office number above  

**Source URLs (verified January 15, 2026):**
- https://www.hcwc.org/get-help/  
- https://www.hcwc.org/get-help/individual-counseling/
