# SECTION 4: HEALTHCARE ACCESS

## Federally Qualified Health Centers (FQHCs)

**Key FQHC Features:**
- Cannot deny services due to inability to pay
- Sliding fee scale based on income and family size
- Accept Medicare, Medicaid, and most insurance
- Offer assistance with coverage enrollment (varies by clinic)

### Central Health – MAP (Medical Access Program) (Travis County)

**Phone:** (512) 978-8130, option 1 (Mon–Fri 8:00 a.m.–5:00 p.m.)
**Apply online:** https://ola.veritysource.com/austin
**Website:** https://www.centralhealth.net/map/

**What it is:**
- Central Health's Medical Access Program (MAP) provides access to healthcare for qualifying Travis County residents.
- MAP and MAP Basic are **not health insurance** and are **not federally funded**.
- Enrolling in MAP will **not** impact your path to a green card.

**Eligibility (income limits at or below 200% FPL):**
- 1 person: $2,608.33/month
- 2 people: $3,525.00/month
- 3 people: $4,441.67/month
- 4 people: $5,358.33/month

**Services covered:**
- Doctor visits, dental care, prescription medications
- Specialty care (cardiology, gastroenterology, psychiatry, etc.)
- Diagnostic services

**How to apply / get screened:**
1. Apply online at https://ola.veritysource.com/austin, OR
2. Call (512) 978-8130, option 1, OR
3. Download application and mail to: Medical Access Program, PO BOX 300489, Austin, TX 78703
4. If you already get care at a local clinic, ask if they can help you with MAP screening/enrollment
5. For general assistance, call **2-1-1**

**Source URLs (verified January 15, 2026):**
- https://www.centralhealth.net/map/
- https://www.211texas.org/about-2-1-1/
