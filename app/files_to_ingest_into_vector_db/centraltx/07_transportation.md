## Capital Metro Discounted Fares (CapMetro)

### Equifare (Low-Income & Social Services Discount)
- **Website:** https://www.capmetro.org/fares-passes/equifare  
- **Apply Online:** https://app.capmetro.org/Equifare  
- **Eligibility (either one):**
  - **Household income below 200% FPL**, **OR**
  - Enrollment in one or more of these programs:
    - Medicaid
    - Telephone Lifeline Program
    - SNAP
    - WIC
    - CHIP
    - SSI
    - HMIS
    - VASH
    - Travis County CEAP
    - City of Austin Utilities CAP
    - Medical Access Program (MAP / MAP Basic)
- **How it Works:**
  - If approved, enrollment lasts **24 months**.
  - Requires the **Umo app** or a **CapMetro Reloadable Fare Card** (existing older physical cards are not accepted).
  - **Pick up a CapMetro Reloadable Fare Card:** CapMetro Transit Store (1705 Guadalupe St, Austin, TX 78701)
- **Help / Questions (CapMetro GO Line):** (512) 474-1200 | customer.service@capmetro.org
- **Equifare Questions:** Equifare@capmetro.org
- **Transit Store:** (512) 389-7454 | Weekdays 8am–5pm  

### Reduced Fare ID (RFID) Card (Half-Price Fare)
- **Website:** https://www.capmetro.org/rfid  
- **Cost:** $3 (valid for **2 years**)  
- **Who is Eligible:**
  - Seniors **65+**
  - Medicare card holders
  - Active/reserve duty military ID holders **not in uniform** (military in uniform ride free)
  - Riders with qualifying disabilities
- **How to Apply / Get Forms:**
  - **Online application** is available for seniors, Medicare card holders, students (18 and under), and active/reserve military.
  - Riders with disabilities must use the **PDF application** (see RFID page).
  - **Request forms by mail:** (512) 389-7475 | requestformailouts@capmetro.org  
- **Renewals / Replacement:** Visit the Transit Store (1705 Guadalupe St). Reduced Fare + Access ID photo hours are typically **weekdays 8:30am–4:30pm** (see CapMetro page for current details).

### HMIS Integrated Fare Program (No-Cost Transit Cards for People in HMIS)
- **Website:** https://www.capmetro.org/hmis-program  
- **What It Is:** CapMetro Reloadable Fare Cards distributed (at no cost) through participating providers for people experiencing homelessness or housing insecurity who are registered in the Austin/Travis County HMIS system.
- **How to Access:** Ask your HMIS service provider/case manager if they participate and can provide a card.
- **Provider Enrollment / Questions:** transitstore.mailbox@capmetro.org  

### Kids 18 and Under Ride Free
- **Website:** https://www.capmetro.org/how-to-ride/riding-with-kids
- **Policy:** Kids **18 and under ride free**. K–12 students ride free with a valid school ID (students can get a CapMetro Student ID at the Transit Store if needed).
- **Safety Note:** Children 10 and under must be accompanied by someone 12 or older (see CapMetro policy page).

**Source URLs (verified January 15, 2026):**

- https://www.capmetro.org/rfid
- https://www.capmetro.org/hmis-program

## CARTS (Capital Area Rural Transportation System)

**Website:** https://www.ridecarts.com/
CARTS provides public transit across rural Central Texas, including on-demand and fixed-route options that connect surrounding counties to Austin and other regional hubs.

### Reservations / General Help
- **Reservations Center:** (512) 478-7433
- **Toll-Free:** (800) 456-7433
- **Main Office:** (512) 481-1011
- **Reservation Center Hours:** Mon–Fri 7:00am–4:30pm  
- **Request a Ride (Online Form):** https://www.ridecarts.com/contact/request-a-ride/  
  - *Note:* Your ride is not booked until a CARTS reservationist confirms it. For next-day rides, call the reservation center.

### Fares (Zones)
- **Website:** https://www.ridecarts.com/fares-passes/  
- **Typical Fares (check site for service-specific details):**
  - Zone 1: $2 (within a town/city)
  - Zone 2: $4 (within the same county)
  - Zone 3: $6 (all-day pass for travel within multiple counties)
- **Half-Price Eligibility:** Registered CARTS customers, persons with disabilities, and children under 12.
- **FREE rides:** Seniors 60+ ride free on all Country Bus services. Veterans ride free when traveling to VA or medical appointments (show Military ID).

### Interurban Coach + "Grasshopper" Connections (Into Austin)
- **Interurban Coach Info:** https://www.ridecarts.com/service/interurban-coach/
- **Route Connections (as listed by CARTS):** Austin–Bastrop–Lockhart–Luling, Round Rock, San Marcos (incl. Texas State University), Taylor, and Austin-Bergstrom International Airport.
- **Service Update (Jan 2026):** Interurban Coach 1517 Express Gold route resumes January 20, 2026.
- **Grasshopper Service:** Registered CARTS customers riding into Austin on Interurban Coach routes may be able to schedule a connecting "Grasshopper" ride to destinations in Austin (advance booking required; eligibility requirements apply; subject to availability).

**Source URLs (verified January 15, 2026):**
- https://www.ridecarts.com/
- https://www.ridecarts.com/fares-passes/
- https://www.ridecarts.com/service/interurban-coach/

## Round Rock Rides (On-Demand Transit)

- **Website:** https://www.roundrocktexas.gov/city-departments/transportation/public-transportation/
- **Book Online:** https://book.roundrock.rideco.com
- **Phone:** (512) 312-2124
- **What It Is:** City-subsidized on-demand rideshare service powered by zTrip. Works similarly to Uber/Lyft—book a ride via app, website, or phone and get picked up at designated locations.
- **Service Area:** Entire Round Rock city limits
- **Service Hours:**
  - Mon–Fri: 6am–8pm
  - Sat: 8am–6pm
  - Closed Sundays and major holidays
- **Fares:**
  - **Standard:** $4 one-way
  - **Discounted ($2 one-way):** Seniors 65+, paratransit-eligible riders, and active-duty military (ID required)
- **Payment:** Exact cash on boarding or prepay via the mobile app
- **Scheduling:** Book as little as 30 minutes ahead or up to 7 days in advance
- **Accessibility:** Curb-to-curb service available for paratransit-eligible riders

**Source URLs (verified January 15, 2026):**
- https://www.roundrocktexas.gov/city-departments/transportation/public-transportation/

## rideGTX (Georgetown Subsidized Rideshare)

- **Website:** https://georgetowntexas.gov/transportation/transit/ridegtx.php
- **What It Is:** City of Georgetown program partnering with **Uber** and **zTrip** to provide subsidized rides 24/7, year-round. Launched May 2025.
- **Eligibility:** Anyone requesting a ride within the service area (not limited to Georgetown residents)
- **Service Area:** Georgetown city limits **plus** Baylor Scott & White and Ascension Seton Williamson hospitals in Round Rock

### Uber Rides
- **How to Book:** Download the Uber app or call **1-833-USE-UBER** (1-833-873-8237). Download monthly vouchers via rideGTX website.
- **Cost:**
  - Up to **10 discounted rides per month**
  - Rider pays the first **$4** per trip
  - City subsidizes up to **$10** per ride
  - Rider pays any remaining balance over $14
- **Eligible Services:** UberX, UberXL, UberX Share (premium services like Uber Black are excluded)
- **Tips:** Paid separately; not covered by vouchers

### zTrip Rides (Wheelchair Accessible)
- **How to Book:** Call **(512) 337-9796** (24/7 call center)
- **Cost:** **$4 per trip** (unlimited rides in service area)
- **Accessibility:** Ramps available for boarding; service animals accepted

### Additional Notes
- Teens 13–17 can use with parental permission (via Uber Teen)
- Service animals accepted on all rides
- Available **24/7, 365 days a year**

**Source URLs (verified January 15, 2026):**
- https://georgetowntexas.gov/transportation/transit/ridegtx.php

## Lyft Jobs Access Program (Employment Transportation)

- **Website:** https://www.lyft.com/lyftup/jobs-access
- **Check Eligibility / Apply:** https://www.lyft.com/lyftup/jobs-access/get-help
- **What It Is:** National program providing **free or discounted Lyft rides** to job seekers through partnerships with **Goodwill®**, **United Way**, and **Indeed**. Operates in 35+ markets across the U.S.
- **Rides Covered:**
  - Job interviews
  - Job training programs
  - First **3 weeks of work** (until first paycheck)
  - Criminal record-expungement services (select locations)
  - Pickup of Indeed-donated internet hotspots
- **How to Access:** Fill out the eligibility form on Lyft's website (link above). A local partner organization will follow up if rides are available in your area.
- **Local Partner:** Goodwill Industries of Central Texas
- **Note:** Program availability and specific offerings **vary by location** depending on local partner funding. Contact Goodwill Central Texas or check the Lyft eligibility form for current Austin-area availability.

**Source URLs (verified January 15, 2026):**
- https://www.lyft.com/lyftup/jobs-access
- https://www.lyft.com/lyftup/jobs-access/get-help

## Texas Medicaid Non-Emergency Medical Transportation (NEMT)

### Nonemergency Medical Transportation Program (HHSC) / Medical Transportation Program (TMHP)
- **HHSC Overview:** https://www.hhs.texas.gov/services/health/medicaid-chip/medicaid-chip-members/nonemergency-medical-transportation-program  
- **TMHP (MTP) Overview:** https://www.tmhp.com/programs/mtp  
- **How to Request a Ride (General Rule):**
  - Call at least **2 workdays** before your appointment (**5 workdays** if the appointment is outside your county).
  - Calls are taken **Mon–Fri, 8am–5pm** (local time).
- **MTP Services Request Line:** (877) 633-8747 (877-MED-TRIP)  
- **TMHP Contact Center:** (800) 925-9126  
- **Same-Day Requests (Limited):** HHSC notes same-day rides may be possible in certain situations (example: urgent same-day need, discharge pickup, pharmacy trip). See HHSC page for specifics.

**Source URLs (verified January 15, 2026):**
- https://www.hhs.texas.gov/services/health/medicaid-chip/medicaid-chip-members/nonemergency-medical-transportation-program
- https://www.tmhp.com/programs/mtp
