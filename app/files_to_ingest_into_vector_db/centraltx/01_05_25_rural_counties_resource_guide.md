CARTS (CAPITAL AREA RURAL TRANSPORTATION SYSTEM)

Counties Served: Bastrop, Blanco, Burnet, Caldwell, Fayette, Hays, Lee, Travis (rural), Williamson (rural)

Contact Information:
Phone: 512-478-RIDE (7433) or 800-456-7433
Website: https://www.ridecarts.com
App: CARTS App (available on App Store & Google Play)
Reservation Hours: Mon-Fri 7am-4:30pm

Fares:
Zone 1 (Within same city/town): $2 one-way, $1 reduced fare
Zone 2 (Within same county): $4 one-way, $2 reduced fare
Zone 3 (Between counties): $6 one-way, $3 reduced fare

Reduced Fare applies to: Seniors 65+, persons with disabilities (with approved application), children under 12

FREE Transportation:
- Seniors 60+: All Country Bus services are FREE
- Veterans: FREE when traveling to VA or medical appointments (show Military ID)
- Personal Care Attendants: FREE when accompanying eligible rider

Services:
- Country Bus: Curb-to-curb service in rural areas; 24-hour advance reservation required; Mon-Fri 8am-4pm
- CARTS NOW: On-demand rides in Bastrop, Lockhart, Marble Falls, Taylor (via app or phone); rides within 15 minutes
- Interurban Coach: Regional intercity routes connecting Austin, Bastrop, Lockhart, Marble Falls, La Grange, Giddings, and other cities

How to Apply for Reduced Fare:
1. Download Disability and Discount Eligibility Form from ridecarts.com/services/disability
2. Have form completed by physician or qualified medical professional
3. Submit by mail

Source URLs:
https://www.ridecarts.com
https://www.ridecarts.com/about/

COMBINED COMMUNITY ACTION INC. (CCA)

Counties Served: Austin, Bastrop, Blanco (weatherization only), Caldwell, Colorado, Fayette, Fort Bend, Hays, Lee

Main Office:
Address: 165 W. Austin St (Hwy 290), Giddings TX 78942, Lee County
Phone: 979-540-2980 or 800-688-9065
CEAP Line: 979-540-2987 (for Austin, Bastrop, Colorado, Fayette, Lee)
Website: https://www.ccaction.com
Hours: Mon-Thu 8am-5pm; Fri 8am-3pm

Programs & Services:

COMPREHENSIVE ENERGY ASSISTANCE PROGRAM (CEAP)
What It Is: Utility bill assistance for electricity, gas, propane
Eligibility:
  - Must reside in service area
  - Income must be at or below 200% of federal poverty level
  - Priority given to: Seniors 60+, persons with disabilities, households with children under 5
What to Bring:
  - Completed CEAP application (download from website or pick up in person)
  - Current utility bill
  - Proof of income for all household members
  - Photo ID
  - Proof of citizenship/legal residency
How to Apply:
  1. Download application from ccaction.com, OR
  2. Call CEAP Line to have application mailed/emailed, OR
  3. Pick up application at Giddings office or partner locations (Advocacy Outreach, community centers)
  4. Submit completed application with all documents

WEATHERIZATION ASSISTANCE PROGRAM
What It Is: Free energy efficiency improvements for your home
Eligibility: Income-eligible homeowners or renters; home must be structurally sound
Services Include: Air sealing, insulation, weather stripping, caulking
Does NOT Include: Repairs/replacement of HVAC units, roof repair, cosmetic renovations
Contact: Carol Nolen, Weatherization Manager - 979-540-2980

TENANT-BASED RENTAL ASSISTANCE (TBRA)
What It Is: Rental subsidies for up to 24 months
Eligibility:
  - Income at or below 80% of area median income
  - Must participate in self-sufficiency plan and case management
Includes: Security deposit assistance, monthly rental subsidies
Phone: 979-540-2984 or 979-540-2997

MEALS ON WHEELS RURAL CAPITAL AREA
What It Is: Hot noon meals for seniors
Eligibility: Age 60+ and spouse
Donation: $2.50 suggested per meal (no one denied if unable to pay)
Congregate Sites: Carmine, Giddings, La Vista, Luling, McDade, San Marcos, Smithville
How to Enroll: Call to schedule intake appointment
Note: Currently has waitlist

CCA TRANSPORTATION BUS
Phone: 800-284-7433
Service Area: Austin, Bastrop, Caldwell, Colorado, Fayette, Lee counties

KEY GAPS IN RURAL COUNTIES

Understanding service gaps helps clients plan ahead:

HOUSING
- No public housing authority in Blanco County (only Texas Housing Foundation)
- No public housing authority in Lee County (only private subsidized housing)
- Long waitlists at most housing authorities (6-24 months typical)
- Limited emergency shelter outside Family Crisis Center and Highland Lakes Family Crisis Center

Note: Bastrop, Burnet, Caldwell (Lockhart and Luling), and Fayette (La Grange) have housing authorities

HEALTHCARE
- No dental services in Blanco or Burnet Counties (travel to La Grange for Tejas dental)
- Limited specialty care (travel to Austin or San Antonio)
- Tejas Health Care serves Bastrop, Fayette, and Lee (dental services at La Grange location only)

EMPLOYMENT
- No Workforce Solutions physical office in Blanco, Fayette, or Lee Counties
- Limited broadband internet in some areas

TRANSPORTATION
- CARTS is primary option with limited schedules
- No ride-share (Uber/Lyft) in most rural areas
- Long distances between services

MENTAL HEALTH & CRISIS
- Bluebonnet Trails does NOT serve Blanco County directly
- Highland Lakes Family Crisis Center does NOT serve Bastrop, Caldwell, Fayette, or Lee Counties
- Family Crisis Center does NOT serve Blanco, Burnet, or Caldwell Counties
- Limited inpatient/residential crisis options (nearest in Georgetown or Seguin)

ORGANIZATIONS THAT DO NOT EXIST OR ARE CLOSED
- Lost Pines Habitat for Humanity (does not exist - Austin Habitat serves the area)
- Caldwell County Housing Authority (does not exist - Lockhart and Luling Housing Authorities serve county)
- Lee County Housing Authority (does not exist - state programs and private subsidized housing only)
- The Refuge, Caldwell County (CLOSED in 2022)
- Faith Academy Bastrop (not found)
