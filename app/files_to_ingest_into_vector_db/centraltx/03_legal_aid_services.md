## General Legal Aid

### Texas RioGrande Legal Aid (TRLA)

**Toll-Free Intake:** (833) 329-8752  
**Intake Hours:** Mon–Fri 9:00 a.m.–5:00 p.m. (Central)  
**Austin Office:** 4920 N I-35, Austin, TX 78751  
**Website:** https://www.trla.org  

**What they help with:**
Free **civil** legal services in 45+ practice areas, including:
- Family law (divorce, custody, protective orders)
- Housing (evictions, landlord-tenant disputes, habitability)
- Employment and wage issues
- Public benefits (SNAP, Medicaid, SSI appeals)
- Immigration
- Consumer issues and debt
- Disaster assistance
- Taxes (through Texas Taxpayer Assistance Project)
- Wills and estate planning
- Small business and nonprofit assistance

**Income eligibility:**
- Generally at or below **125% of Federal Poverty Guidelines**
- Disaster-affected households: up to **200% FPG**
- At least one household member must be a U.S. citizen or Legal Permanent Resident (LPR)

**How to get help:**
1. Call the toll-free intake line during business hours to apply
2. If calling about a **family-law** problem, call on **Tuesdays and Thursdays only**
3. For non-English/Spanish speakers, TRLA's main intake line offers a **language line**
4. You can also submit an initial screening form online for some services
5. If denied, TRLA lists an appeal process on their "Get Help" page

**Note:** Due to limited resources, TRLA can only represent a limited number of clients in court. They try to provide at least basic advice to all eligible callers.

**Source URLs (verified January 15, 2026):**
- https://www.trla.org/help
- https://www.trla.org/offices

## Tenant / Housing Legal Services

### Austin Tenants Council (ATC) – A TRLA Project

**Phone:** (512) 474-1961
**Website:** https://www.trla.org/atc/

**Telephone Counseling Hours:**
- Monday–Thursday: 9:00 a.m.–12:00 p.m., 1:00 p.m.–4:00 p.m.
- Friday: 9:00 a.m.–12:00 p.m.

**What they help with:**
- Telephone counseling on tenant rights and the eviction process
- Review of eviction notices and lease documents
- Mediation services for landlord-tenant disputes
- Housing discrimination assistance
- In-house counseling (eligibility may be limited to Travis County residents meeting income requirements)
- Lease forms and tenant rights information

**Topics covered:**
- Eviction process and defenses
- Security deposit disputes
- Repair and remedy rights
- Landlord retaliation protections
- Fair housing rights

**Note:** Housing advocates provide guidance but are not attorneys and cannot offer legal advice.

**Source URLs (verified January 15, 2026):**
- https://www.trla.org/atc/
- https://texaslawhelp.org/directory/austin-tenants-council

### BASTA (Building and Strengthening Tenant Action) – A TRLA Project

**Website:** https://bastaaustin.org/  
**Tenants Rights Info:** https://bastaaustin.org/en/tenants-rights  

**What they do:**
- Help Austin renters work together to improve housing conditions
- Support tenants associations at multifamily properties
- Provide know-your-rights education and presentations
- Offer training, educational materials, and legal support for tenant groups
- Individual tenant representation in eviction defense, habitability issues, and ledger disputes

**Services include:**
- Interactive know-your-rights presentations
- How to file a code complaint guides
- Reasonable accommodation request assistance
- Community organizing support
- Legal representation (limited capacity)

**Best for:** Tenants who want to organize with neighbors, tenants associations, or renters facing habitability/repair issues

**Source URLs (verified January 15, 2026):**
- https://bastaaustin.org/
- https://bastaaustin.org/en/tenants-rights

### City of Austin Office of Civil Rights – Fair Housing

**Phone:** (512) 974-3251  
**File a complaint:** https://www.austintexas.gov/department/office-equity-and-inclusion/civil-rights  
**Website:** https://www.austintexas.gov/page/tenant-stabilization-and-renters-rights  

**What they help with:**
- Enforces Austin's Fair Housing Ordinance
- Investigates housing discrimination complaints

**Protected classes under Austin's Fair Housing Ordinance:**
- All federal protections (race, color, national origin, religion, sex, familial status, disability)
- **Plus:** sexual orientation, gender identity, marital status, student status, and age
