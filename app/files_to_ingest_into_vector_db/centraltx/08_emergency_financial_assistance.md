## Rental Assistance Programs

### I Belong in Austin (IBIA) – City of Austin Rental Assistance Program

**Program Administrator:** El Buen Samaritano
**Phone:** (512) 439-8900 (Mon–Fri 8:00 a.m.–5:00 p.m.)
**Email:** hello@elbuen.org
**Apply Online:** https://elbuen.org/ibia/
**City of Austin Program Page:** https://www.austintexas.gov/rent
**Address:** 7000 Woodhue Drive, Austin, TX 78745

**What it is:**
- City of Austin tenant rental assistance program for residents vulnerable to eviction
- Provides financial assistance with rent, moving, storage, and relocation costs to help avoid eviction

**How the application process works:**
1. Application portal opens the **first week of each month** (typically opens on the 1st at 8:00 a.m. and closes on the 7th at 5:00 p.m.)
2. Recipients are chosen using a **lottery system** (randomized selection) until funding is exhausted
3. If selected, El Buen Samaritano staff will contact you for eligibility verification and documentation
4. **Applications do not carry over** to the next month; reapply when the portal reopens if not selected
5. Payments are made **directly to landlords/vendors** (landlord participation required)

**Eligibility requirements:**
- Must have an **eviction notice, letter to vacate, or court number and date**
- Housing must be located in the **City of Austin full-purpose jurisdiction**
- Household income must not exceed **80% of Area Median Family Income (AMFI)**:

| Household Size | 1 Person | 2 People | 3 People | 4 People | 5 People | 6 People | 7 People | 8 People |
|----------------|----------|----------|----------|----------|----------|----------|----------|----------|
| 80% AMFI Limit | $68,500 | $78,250 | $88,050 | $97,800 | $105,650 | $113,450 | $121,300 | $129,100 |

**Required documentation (if selected):**
- Proof of identification (photo ID, government ID, passport, school ID, or public assistance card)
- Income documentation (paystub, employer letter, tax docs, or proof of SNAP/Medicaid benefits)
- Documentation showing eviction risk (notice from landlord, intent to vacate notice, legal referral, or ledger with outstanding balance)

**Important notes:**
- Submit **only one application per household** (multiple applications will not increase chances)
- Processing time is generally up to **15 business days** after documentation is complete
- Application assistance available in **multiple languages**

**Source URLs (verified January 15, 2026):**
- https://www.austintexas.gov/rent
- https://elbuen.org/ibia/

### Front Steps – First Steps Housing Stability Program

**Address:** 4507 North Interstate 35, Austin, TX 78722  
**Phone:** (512) 696-1484  
**Email:** info@frontsteps.org  
**Website:** https://frontsteps.org/first-steps-homeless-prevention/  
**Apply Online:** https://frontsteps.org/apply-now/

**Current status:** Program is **closed** and will reopen **March 31, 2026**.

**What it is:**
- One-time financial assistance to help individuals and families stay in their homes and avoid homelessness
- May cover past-due rent, security deposits, utilities, pet fees, and other housing-related costs
- Payments go directly to landlords or utility companies

**Service area:** Austin Metro Area (Travis County and parts of Williamson County)

**Eligibility requirements:**
- Must be **18 years or older**
- Must live in the **Austin Metro Area** (Travis County or parts of Williamson County)
- Must be at **imminent risk of homelessness** (will lose housing within 14 days of applying)
- Must have **no other housing options** and lack funds to find another permanent place
- Must show you have taken steps to resolve the situation on your own

**How it works:**
1. Submit application online (when portal is open)
2. A case manager will contact you within **14 business days**
3. If approved, payments are sent to landlord/utility company within **10–14 business days**
4. **One application only** — submitting multiple applications slows the process

**Important notes:**
- Applying does **not guarantee approval**
- Assistance is one-time per year
- Program serves people losing a home they own/rent, being asked to leave a friend/family's home, or being made to leave a place where they don't pay rent

**Source URLs (verified January 15, 2026):**
- https://frontsteps.org/first-steps-homeless-prevention/

### Austin Public Health (APH) Neighborhood Centers – Rent & Utility Help

**Rent & Utility Assistance Line:** (512) 972-5780  
**Website:** https://www.austintexas.gov/services/get-help-neighborhood-centers  
**Hours:** Phone lines open at 8:00 a.m. on the first day of each week; slots fill quickly

**Important update:** APH **no longer accepts walk-ins** for rent or Austin Energy assistance. All requests must go through the Rent & Utility line.

**What they help with:**
- Rent assistance (past-due rent only)
- Austin Energy utility assistance

**Eligibility requirements:**
- Must be experiencing a **financial crisis**
- Must be a resident of **Austin/Travis County**
- Must be a **renter with your name on the lease or contract**
- Rent/utility bill must be **PAST DUE**
- Household income must be at or below **200% Federal Poverty Income Guidelines (FPIG)**
- Must have **proof of recent crisis** (job loss, reduced hours, medical bills, household changes, funeral expenses, etc.)

**How it works:**
1. Call (512) 972-5780 when lines open (8:00 a.m. on Mondays)
2. Phone screening does **not guarantee** assistance but starts the process
3. Appointments are set in the order calls are received
4. Automated message will inform callers when slots are filled for the week or when funds are exhausted

**Active Neighborhood Center locations:**
- **Dove Springs:** 5811 Palo Blanco Lane, Austin, TX 78744 | (512) 972-6699
