## Crisis & Immediate Support

### Austin/Travis County Sobering Center

**Phone:** (512) 957-1900  
**Address:** 1213 Sabine Street, Austin, TX 78701  
**Hours:** Open 24/7  
**Website:** https://soberingcenter.org/

**What it is:**
A safe place for publicly intoxicated individuals to sober up as an alternative to the emergency room or jail. Free, voluntary, and confidential.

**How to access:**
- Must be referred by law enforcement, EMS, or approved partner agencies
- Call (512) 957-1900 — staff will determine if someone is eligible
- Does not accept walk-ups
- Must be 18+ and medically stable

**Services provided:**
- Medical screening at intake
- Safe place to sleep and sober up (typically 4–8 hours)
- Snacks and hydration
- SBIRT counseling (Screening, Brief Intervention, Referral to Treatment)
- Connections to treatment and recovery resources
- Holdover program for extended stabilization (expanding in 2025–2026)

**Key facts:**
- No cost, citations, fines, or medical bills
- Complete confidentiality — no one is notified
- Over 13,000 people served since 2018
- Reduces jail bookings and emergency room visits

### Certification Process Overview

**Certifying body:** Texas Certification Board

**Process:**
1. Complete formal training with a certified training entity
2. Complete a supervised internship period
3. Receive supervision from a Peer Specialist Supervisor
4. Apply to Texas Certification Board for full certification

**Timeline:** Full certification can be achieved within 8 months, but may take up to 1 year

**Renewal:** Every 2 years with 20 continuing education hours. Fee: $60 plus background check fee.

**More information:** https://www.hhs.texas.gov/providers/behavioral-health-services-providers/peer-support-services/certification-peer-support-a-medicaid-benefit

**Who can access:**
- Adults 18+ in recovery from substance use or co-occurring conditions
- Family members, friends, and allies welcome
- No fee for services; no eligibility requirements for most programs

**Travis County Pre-Trial Diversion:**
- Communities for Recovery hosts approved classes for diversion participants
- Group tour orientations: 2nd Friday and 4th Monday of each month at 1:00 p.m.

## Tips for Getting Help

1. **Start with a crisis line if you're unsure** — Integral Care (512-472-4357) can help you figure out the right next step.

2. **Know your insurance** — Many treatment programs accept Medicaid, Medicare, and private insurance. Ask about sliding scale fees if uninsured.

3. **Consider your level of need:**
   - Detox (medical supervision for withdrawal)
   - Residential/inpatient (live at facility)
   - Intensive outpatient (IOP) — several hours/week
   - Standard outpatient — weekly sessions
   - Peer support — ongoing community connection

4. **Peer support is free** — Communities for Recovery and 12-step meetings cost nothing and welcome everyone.

5. **Recovery housing helps** — Sober living environments significantly improve long-term outcomes.

6. **Involve your support system** — Many programs offer family education and counseling.

7. **Relapse is common but not required** — If you've tried before and it didn't work, try again. Different approaches work for different people.
